# Person Dossier Template

Every generated person dossier follows this structure. A section is omitted
entirely (not shown as empty) if nothing at ≥0.9 confidence was found for it —
never fabricated or padded with hedging filler. See `RESEARCH_PLAN.md` for
which sources feed each section and the confidence scoring rubric.

---

```markdown
# {{Full Name}}
**{{Current Title}} — {{Organization Name}}**

*Dossier generated {{date}} · {{N}} sources reviewed · {{N}} claims at ≥0.9 confidence*

## Executive Summary
2-4 sentences, ESB/AJ voice. What a practitioner needs to know walking in:
who this person is, their track record, and anything they should be aware
of before the first conversation.

## Current Role
- **Title:** ...
- **Organization:** ...
- **Start date / tenure length:** ...
- **Term / contract details:** (elected term length + next election date,
  or contract end date, if a hired position)
- **Reports to / governance relationship:** (e.g. "reports to the elected
  board" for a superintendent; "represents District 3, elected [date]" for
  a board member)

## Career History
Reverse-chronological. Each entry: role, organization, dates, one-line
description of scope/scale (e.g. "Superintendent, 12,000-student district").

## Education
Degree, field, institution, year (only where individually confirmed —
never inferred from a "matches a common pattern" guess).
Certifications/licenses (e.g. state superintendent credential) if found.

## Track Record & Public Positions
For a superintendent: initiatives launched, stated priorities, measurable
outcomes where publicly reported (test scores, graduation rates trending
under their tenure — cited to the specific report, not vibes).
For a board member: voting record on notable items, committee assignments,
publicly stated priorities/platform (if elected on one).

## Public Statements & Media Presence
Direct quotes, op-eds, interviews — each with source and date. This section
exists to give a sense of how the person talks about their work, not just
what they've done.

## News Coverage Timeline
Chronological list of notable coverage — both favorable and unfavorable.
Each entry: date, outlet, one-line summary, link.

## Areas of Scrutiny
Anything adversarial found in the public record: lawsuits, votes of no
confidence, formal complaints, resignations under pressure, disciplinary
action, state/local investigations. This is the section practitioners most
need and most often have to dig for themselves — be thorough, but every
claim here needs a real source; this is not the place to relax the
confidence bar.

## Campaign Finance & Political Activity
*(elected officials only)* Major donors, PAC involvement, opponents in
last election, margin of victory. Sourced to FollowTheMoney/OpenSecrets/
state disclosure portal — never estimated.

## Professional Network & Affiliations
Other board memberships, professional associations (e.g. AASA, state
superintendents' association), notable prior colleagues who are also
relevant to ESB's work with this person.

## Public Financial / Property Footprint
*(only if found via public records — property ownership, business
filings, other directorships. Never home address — organization/
jurisdiction only, e.g. "owns commercial property in [county]," not a
street address.)*

## Historical Note
Anything found via Wayback Machine that differs from the current live
record — an old bio that was edited, a staff page that quietly dropped
someone, a policy position that changed. Flagged here because it's often
the most revealing category and the easiest to miss without deliberately
checking archives.

## Sources
Every source consulted, with the confidence tier assigned and whether it
contributed a claim used above. This is the audit trail — link, tier,
date accessed.

## Research Gaps
What was searched for but not found, or found only below the confidence
threshold. Honesty about gaps is part of the deliverable — a practitioner
should know what's still unknown, not just what's known.
```
