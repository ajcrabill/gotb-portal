# Organization (District) Dossier Template

Same omit-don't-pad rule as the person template — see `RESEARCH_PLAN.md`
for sourcing and confidence scoring.

---

```markdown
# {{District Name}} ({{State}})

*Dossier generated {{date}} · {{N}} sources reviewed · {{N}} claims at ≥0.9 confidence*

## Executive Summary
2-4 sentences: what kind of district this is, where it stands, and
anything a practitioner should know before a first conversation with
its leadership.

## Basic Profile
- **NCES LEA ID:** ...
- **Enrollment:** ... (with year, and trend direction if known)
- **Number of schools:** ...
- **Locale type:** (urban/suburban/rural, per NCES classification)
- **District type:** (unified/elementary/secondary/etc.)
- **CGCS member:** yes/no

## Governance Structure
- **Board composition:** number of seats, at-large vs. single-member
  districts, election method, term lengths
- **Current board members:** name, seat/district, term expiration
  (cross-reference to person dossiers where they exist)
- **Superintendent:** name, start date, contract end date
  (cross-reference to their person dossier)

## Recent Leadership History
Superintendent turnover (dates, reasons where publicly known), major
board composition changes, recent election results. This is often the
single most useful section — high turnover or a recent contentious
election is exactly the kind of context a practitioner needs before
walking in.

## Financial Health
Revenue/expenditure trends (NCES School District Finance Survey),
recent bond measures (passed/failed, amount, purpose), any publicly
reported financial distress signals (state financial oversight,
credit rating changes, budget shortfalls covered in local news).

## Academic Performance
State report-card data: test performance, graduation rate, and their
multi-year trend — cited to the specific state report card, not a
national aggregator's summary of it.

## Demographic & Enrollment Trends
Growth/decline over recent years, socioeconomic indicators (free/
reduced lunch %, if published), any relevant demographic shifts
driving governance pressure (e.g. rapid growth requiring new schools,
declining enrollment forcing closures).

## Strategic Priorities
Current strategic plan goals if publicly published, stated board
priorities, any major initiatives underway.

## Areas of Scrutiny
Lawsuits, state takeovers or oversight, votes of no confidence in
leadership, walkouts/strikes, high-profile policy fights (book bans,
boundary changes, etc.), state or federal investigations. Same
standard as the person template: thorough, but every claim sourced.

## Media Coverage Timeline
Chronological, notable local and (if applicable) national coverage.

## Community Context
Local political environment relevant to governance (e.g. union
relationship, notable community organizations active in district
politics, recent contentious elections beyond the school board).

## Historical Note
Wayback-sourced findings that differ from the current live record —
removed pages, edited strategic plans, quietly dropped initiatives.

## Sources
Full audit trail, same format as the person template.

## Research Gaps
What's still unknown.
```
